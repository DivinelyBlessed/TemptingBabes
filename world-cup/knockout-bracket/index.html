New for 2026: The Round of 32 is the first entirely new knockout stage introduced since the World Cup expanded to 32 teams in 1998. The 32 qualifiers are the group winners (12), runners-up (12) and the best 8 third-placed teams. The bracket is pre-seeded based on group performance — meaning the bracket is set from day one and unfolds match by match.

The introduction of the Round of 32 fundamentally changes the tournament's drama curve. In previous editions, the knockout rounds began with a Round of 16 — only 16 teams remaining. In 2026, 32 teams face single-elimination pressure simultaneously across five days. The sheer volume of high-stakes knockout football in the first week of July will be unlike anything seen at a World Cup before.

For bracket prediction purposes, the draw pairing between group positions matters enormously. The bracket seeds group winners against third-placed teams, and runners-up against runners-up from different sections of the draw. Understanding which half of the bracket your team occupies — and which potential opponents await in the Round of 16 — is the difference between a manageable route and a nightmare path to the final.

Predictions

Who Makes It to the Final?

Based on current squad quality, form and draw positioning, the pre-tournament favourites for a Final appearance are: Spain (defending Euro 2024 champions, youngest and most technically gifted squad), Brazil (Vinicius-led attacking line with genuine depth), France (Mbappé playing for PSG motivation after his domestic trophy drought) and England (strongest squad in their history, desperate to end 60 years of hurt).

Dark horses worth monitoring in the bracket: Morocco (2022 semi-finalists, defensively elite), Portugal (post-Ronaldo era finally arriving), Germany (Nagelsmann's tactical evolution) and the USA as host nation. American crowds at AT&T Stadium and MetLife Stadium will create a home advantage that no statistical model fully captures. An emotional US run to the semi-finals cannot be ruled out.
