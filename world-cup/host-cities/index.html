United States — 11 Host Cities
New York / New Jersey
MetLife Stadium · 82,500 cap
Hosts the Final on July 19. 8 matches total. The tournament's biggest stage — just outside Manhattan.
Dallas / Fort Worth
AT&T Stadium · 80,000 cap
One Semi-Final. 9 matches. Cowboys' stadium — the indoor, climate-controlled venue that eliminates weather concerns.
Los Angeles
SoFi Stadium · 70,240 cap
7 matches including high-profile group games. Entertainment capital — celebrity presence guaranteed.
San Francisco Bay Area
Levi's Stadium · 68,500 cap
6 matches. Home of the San Francisco 49ers. Silicon Valley backdrop.
Miami
Hard Rock Stadium · 64,767 cap
7 matches. Miami's Latin American community makes this one of the most electric atmospheres in the US.
Seattle
Lumen Field · 68,740 cap
6 matches. Pacific Northwest atmosphere. Closest US venue to Vancouver, Canada.
Boston
Gillette Stadium · 65,878 cap
6 matches. Strong Irish and European immigrant community creates genuine football atmosphere.
Philadelphia
Lincoln Financial Field · 69,796 cap
6 matches. Known for passionate sports crowds — a genuinely hostile environment for visiting teams.
Atlanta
Mercedes-Benz Stadium · 71,000 cap
6 matches. One of the newest and most impressive stadiums in US sport. Climate-controlled.
Houston
NRG Stadium · 72,220 cap
6 matches. Texas's massive Latin American community ensures raucous atmospheres. Indoor climate control.
Kansas City
Arrowhead Stadium · 76,416 cap
6 matches. Known as one of the loudest stadiums in American sport.
