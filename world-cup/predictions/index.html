Champions

Germany
+1000 • Resurgent
Resurgent
Germany are back. The chaos of the 2018 and 2022 group-stage exits feels like a distant memory. This squad — young, structured, lethal — have rediscovered German efficiency. Group E features Ivory Coast and Ecuador. If they hit form by the knockouts, they can beat anyone on the day.

+1200 • Dark Contender
Dark Horse
Ronaldo at his last World Cup is a storyline that writes itself. But Portugal are no longer a one-man team — Bruno Fernandes, Rafael Leão and Vitinha power a squad with real depth. Group K faces Colombia and Congo DR. If CR7 has one legendary tournament left in him, Portugal go deep.

Value
Van Dijk's leadership, Gakpo's goals, the width of Dumfries and Malen. The Dutch are tournament football specialists — organised, hard to beat, capable of moments of brilliance. They went to the 2022 quarter-final. Group F is manageable. At +1400 they are the best value of the top contenders.

In 2022, Morocco reached the semi-final — the best African performance in World Cup history. They haven't forgotten how to shock the world. In Group C alongside Brazil, they're already being tested. Navigate that and watch out.

Questions

Predictions FAQ

Who is the favourite to win the 2026 World Cup?
France and Spain are joint favourites with major bookmakers at around +450–+500 as of May 2026. England follow at +600, with Brazil and Argentina further back at +700–+900. The market has tightened significantly since the group draw in December 2025 revealed France and Spain's favourable paths to the final.
Can Argentina defend their World Cup title?
Historically, defending champions rarely win back-to-back. The last team to do so was Brazil in 1958 and 1962. At +900, Argentina are considered a genuine but not likely defender — Messi's age and squad depth without his peak form are the key concerns. Our prediction is a quarter-final exit.
Who will be top scorer at the 2026 World Cup?
Kylian Mbappé is the favourite for the Golden Boot. At his absolute prime at 27, France are likely to go deep into the tournament — giving him maximum matches to score. Bellingham and Vinicius Jr are the main challengers, with Ronaldo a wildcard for his final World Cup.
Which team is the biggest dark horse in 2026?
Morocco are our top dark horse pick. They reached the semi-final in 2022 and have only improved since. If they survive Group C — which also contains Brazil — they have the defensive organisation and counter-attack pace to eliminate any team in the knockout stages.
Will the host nations — USA, Canada, Mexico — perform well?
USA have the best chance of a deep run. Playing at home in front of massive crowds with a favourable Group D draw is a significant advantage. Mexico always perform competitively at home but historically struggle in the Round of 16. Canada are the wildcard — qualifying was an achievement; making the knockout stage would be historic.
Who will England face in the final?
Based on our bracket predictions, England's route sets up a potential semi-final against Spain or France, depending on the quarter-final draw. If they make the final, we see France or Spain on the other side — a Wembley-esque occasion on the grandest stage. England are capable; the question is whether they can overcome their semi-final mentality.
