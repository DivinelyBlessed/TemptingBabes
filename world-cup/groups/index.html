FAQ

Group Stage Questions Answered

Everything you need to know about how the group stage works in the expanded 48-team format.

How does the group stage work in World Cup 2026?
The 48 teams are divided into 12 groups of 4. Each team plays every other team in its group once — 3 matches total. Teams earn 3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss. The top 2 teams in each group automatically advance. The 8 best third-place finishers (ranked across all groups) also advance, giving 32 teams in total heading into the Round of 32.
How many teams advance from each group?
2 teams advance automatically from each group (the top 2). Additionally, the 8 best third-place finishers across all 12 groups also advance. So out of 4 teams per group, 2 always go through, and a 3rd-place finish may also be enough depending on points and goal difference.
When does the World Cup 2026 group stage start and end?
The group stage begins on June 11, 2026 with the opening match — Mexico vs South Africa at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City. The group stage runs through to approximately July 2, 2026, after which the Round of 32 knockout stage begins.
How are tiebreakers decided if teams finish level on points?
If teams finish level on points, the tiebreaker order is: (1) goal difference, (2) goals scored, (3) head-to-head points, (4) head-to-head goal difference, (5) head-to-head goals scored, (6) fair play points (yellow/red cards), (7) FIFA ranking. See the official FIFA tiebreaker rules for full details.
Which is the Group of Death in 2026?
Group C — Brazil, Morocco, Scotland and Haiti — is widely considered the Group of Death. Brazil vs Morocco is arguably the standout match of the entire group stage. Group H (Spain, Uruguay, Saudi Arabia, Cape Verde) and Group I (France, Senegal, Norway, Iraq) are also brutal. Three tough groups out of twelve is unusual even for a 48-team tournament.
Who are the host nations and which groups are they in?
All three host nations qualified automatically: Mexico is in Group A (opening the tournament at Estadio Azteca), Canada is in Group B (playing in Toronto and Vancouver), and the United States is in Group D (playing across multiple stadiums on home soil). Host nations get home crowd advantages but face serious competition in all three groups.
How many matches are in the group stage?
There are 72 group-stage matches in total — each of the 12 groups plays 6 matches (every team plays every other team once). This is a significant increase from the 48 group-stage matches at the 32-team World Cup, and means there's a live game virtually every day of the group stage.
