The world's most storied football stadium hosts its third World Cup opener. Estadio Azteca has witnessed Pelé's greatest performance (1970) and Maradona's Hand of God (1986). Now it sets the stage for 48 nations and a continental celebration. The ceremony will showcase three opening performances—Mexico, USA, and Canada—honoring the first World Cup jointly hosted by three nations. Official song 'Dai Dai' by Shakira & Burna Boy marks the tournament's theme of global unity.

Three Nations, One Celebration

Host Nations Opening Ceremonies

For the first time in World Cup history, three nations share hosting duties. Each will have its own opening ceremony moment during the main event, celebrating continental culture and football heritage.

Mexico's Ceremony: Indigenous traditions, regional music, and the passing of the torch. Estadio Azteca becomes a vessel for 500 years of Mexican culture—from ancient Aztec ceremonial grounds to modern football mecca. Performers will blend traditional instruments with contemporary artists.

USA's Contribution: The ceremonial component celebrating American football culture, diversity, and innovation. Moments honoring the evolution of the sport in North America and the cultural melting pot that defines the USA.

Canada's Moment: Arctic to urban landscapes, indigenous heritage, and the multicultural mosaic that represents Canada. A celebration of hockey culture, natural beauty, and bilingual identity.
