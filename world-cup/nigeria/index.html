Is Nigeria in the World Cup 2026?

No. The Super Eagles did not qualify for the 2026 FIFA World Cup — their second consecutive tournament absence. Here’s the full story.

Direct Answer
No — Nigeria is NOT at the 2026 World Cup

Nigeria failed to qualify for the 2026 FIFA World Cup during the CAF (African) qualification rounds. The Super Eagles were eliminated before reaching the tournament, meaning they will not feature in the USA, Canada & Mexico this summer. This is Nigeria’s second consecutive World Cup absence, having also missed out on Qatar 2022.

The Story

Second Consecutive Absence

Nigeria’s absence from the 2026 World Cup is one of the bigger shocks of the entire qualification process. The Super Eagles are one of Africa’s most storied football nations — three-time Africa Cup of Nations winners, with World Cup appearances in 1994, 1998, 2002, 2010, 2014, and 2018. But they also missed Qatar 2022 after losing a controversial playoff to Ghana on away goals, and they have now compounded that absence by failing to qualify for 2026 as well.

Africa had nine places at the 2026 World Cup — the most the continent has ever received at a single tournament. With that many spots available, Nigeria’s failure to claim one of them is a significant failure for one of the continent’s football powerhouses, and it has left millions of Nigerian fans searching for answers.
