Why It Matters

The World Cup’s Busiest Venue

AT&T Stadium is not just another host venue — it is the tournament’s most active stadium by match count, and the only venue in the 2026 draw that stages games from the opening group stage all the way through to a Semifinal. That continuity makes it the single most strategically important stadium in the entire three-nation footprint.

The venue has a proven record at this scale. It has already hosted the Super Bowl, the NBA All-Star Weekend, and elite international soccer events, demonstrating that it can handle the crowd volume, broadcast infrastructure, and operational complexity that a World Cup demands. For international visitors, that history matters: this is a stadium designed for exactly these moments.

  • 9 Matches — Tournament Record: More than any other 2026 World Cup venue, spanning the full middle stretch from June 14 to July 14.
  • Semifinal on July 14: One of two Semifinal slots in the entire tournament — AT&T Stadium hosts one of the two matches that decide the finalists.
  • Dallas Stadium branding: FIFA’s event-specific naming means searchers find it under AT&T Stadium, Dallas Stadium, Arlington stadium, and World Cup Dallas venue — all the same place.
  • American soccer legacy: North Texas hosted games in the 1994 World Cup, and 2026 brings that legacy back with a much larger format and global audience.

Venue Experience

Inside AT&T Stadium

AT&T Stadium is widely described as one of the world’s most technologically advanced sports venues. The retractable roof means weather is not a factor regardless of Texas summer heat. The center-hung video board — one of the largest in any stadium — gives every seat a clear view of replays and broadcast graphics at a scale that matches the ambition of the World Cup itself.

Standard capacity sits at around 80,000, but the venue has expanded that number for landmark events in the past. For World Cup visitors, this means large, loud, and highly produced — the atmosphere will feel closer to a global finals venue than a normal league ground. That is partly why FIFA has assigned the Semifinal here: the infrastructure supports the occasion.

Logistics

Travel & Parking at Dallas Stadium

Parking and access are among the biggest practical concerns for World Cup visitors heading to AT&T Stadium. Reports ahead of the tournament indicate that parking will be significantly more expensive than a typical Cowboys game — which is already not cheap. For anyone who can plan ahead, booking parking in advance or using transit alternatives will save both money and time.

The venue’s AT&T Stadium signage has been covered over for World Cup branding, reinforcing that this is an operationally transformed event site, not a normal NFL matchday. Crowd management, security lanes, and entry procedures will all reflect FIFA’s tournament-scale protocols. Build in extra time before kickoff.

  • Parking: Expect elevated World Cup pricing — pre-book where possible, or plan an alternative route in.
  • Transport: The TEXRail and TRE rail lines connect the broader Dallas-Fort Worth area to Arlington, reducing parking dependency.
  • Timing: FIFA recommends arriving at least two hours before kickoff for major knockout matches. For the Semifinal on July 14, plan for three hours minimum.
  • Branding: All stadium signage reverts to Dallas Stadium for the tournament — check FIFA’s official venue maps to confirm entry gates.
