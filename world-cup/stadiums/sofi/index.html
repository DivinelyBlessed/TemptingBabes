Architecture

A Building That Looks Like the Future

When Los Angeles Rams owner Stan Kroenke commissioned HKS Architects to build what would become the most expensive sporting venue in human history, the mandate was not to replicate what had come before. It was to make something that would still feel radical in 2028, in 2038, in 2048. Standing inside the completed structure — its translucent ETFE canopy diffusing Southern California light into something soft and golden, the 70,000-square-foot Infinity Screen hanging above the field like a digital halo — it is hard to argue they failed.

World Cup Conversion

What Changes for Soccer

Transforming SoFi from an NFL venue into a FIFA-compliant World Cup site is not a simple task. The FIFA standard pitch dimensions — wider than a standard NFL field — require meaningful modifications to the seating arrangement. The most significant change involves the corner sections: seats must be removed or repositioned to accommodate the wider playing surface, reducing capacity from the NFL standard of 70,240 to the World Cup configuration of 69,650.

  • Natural turf: FIFA requires natural grass for all World Cup matches. SoFi, which uses artificial turf for NFL games, underwent a full pitch replacement as part of a two-phase modernisation process designed to avoid disrupting the NFL schedule.
  • Capacity adjustment: NFL 70,240 → World Cup 69,650. Corner rows removed to accommodate the wider FIFA-standard pitch.
  • FIFA naming: SoFi Stadium becomes Los Angeles Stadium for the duration of the tournament — consistent with FIFA’s sponsorship protection protocol applied to all 16 host venues.
  • Spectator distance: New rows added in each corner to meet the over-73-metres distance required by FIFA from the nearest spectator to the pitch sideline.
Los Angeles

The World’s Soccer City

If you wanted to design a city from scratch to host the World Cup, you might end up with something very close to Los Angeles. No major metropolitan area in the United States has a more passionate, more diverse, or more historically embedded relationship with football than the City of Angels. The Mexican, Central American, and South American diaspora communities that have shaped Los Angeles for generations are also the communities that have shaped the culture of the sport itself.

Los Angeles is home to the largest Mexican diaspora community outside of Mexico itself. It is a city where La Liga results matter at 7am on Sunday mornings, where the Copa América draws genuine street-level electricity, and where LAFC and the LA Galaxy play before crowds that understand the game at a level of tactical sophistication more familiar to European than American sporting culture. SoFi Stadium, with its global technology and its Inglewood address, sits at the intersection of American spectacle and global football identity.

Getting There

Travel & Access Guide

SoFi Stadium’s location in Inglewood — just east of LAX and south of downtown Los Angeles — makes it one of the most accessible major sports venues in the country for international visitors arriving by air. The distance from LAX to the stadium is approximately 3 miles, a journey of roughly 10–15 minutes in typical traffic.

LA Metro Match Day Direct Service

FIFA and LA Metro have collaborated on an enhanced World Cup Match Day Direct Service connecting park-and-ride locations across the metropolitan area to the stadium for all eight fixtures. Fans opting for park-and-ride have their shuttle ticket included in the parking cost — described by transport authorities as the most efficient and cost-effective way to reach the venue on match days.

  • From LAX: ~3 miles — dedicated shuttle service from LAX and the nearest Metro station, operating for all World Cup match days.
  • Public transit: LA Metro Match Day Direct Service runs park-and-ride connections across the metropolitan area.
  • Driving: Hollywood Park campus includes significant parking infrastructure. FIFA strongly recommends public transport for World Cup match days to manage crowd volume.
  • International visitors: SoFi sits within a broader tourism infrastructure — Hollywood, Santa Monica, Beverly Hills, and fan zones across Inglewood will be active throughout the tournament.
  • Quarter-Final timing: For the July 10 Quarter-Final, expect globally distributed crowds from beyond LA. Allow extra time for all access routes.
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How many World Cup 2026 games does SoFi Stadium host?
SoFi Stadium hosts 8 FIFA World Cup 2026 matches in total: 5 group stage games (June 12, 15, 18, 21, and 25), 2 Round of 32 matches in late June, and 1 Quarter-Final on July 10, 2026.
What is SoFi Stadium called during FIFA World Cup 2026?
During FIFA World Cup 2026, SoFi Stadium is officially called Los Angeles Stadium. FIFA applies sponsorship protection rules to all 16 host venues, requiring the use of neutral, city-based names in place of commercial naming rights titles for the duration of the tournament.
How much did SoFi Stadium cost to build?
SoFi Stadium cost approximately $5.5 billion to construct, making it the most expensive stadium ever built at the time of its 2020 opening. The project was 100% privately financed by Los Angeles Rams owner Stan Kroenke, with no public funding from the City of Inglewood or the State of California.
What is SoFi Stadium’s capacity for World Cup 2026?
SoFi Stadium’s World Cup 2026 seating capacity is approximately 69,650. This is slightly lower than the standard NFL configuration of 70,240 because corner seats must be modified to accommodate the wider FIFA-standard soccer pitch dimensions.
Does SoFi Stadium have a roof?
Yes. SoFi Stadium is covered by a translucent ETFE (ethylene tetrafluoroethylene) canopy roof — the only covered venue among all 11 US World Cup 2026 sites. The roof allows natural light to enter while protecting fans from rain and direct sun, creating a distinctive indoor-outdoor atmosphere.
Which USA World Cup games are at SoFi Stadium?
The United States men’s national team plays two group stage matches at SoFi Stadium: USA vs. Paraguay on June 15 and USA vs. Turkey (officially Türkiye in FIFA documentation) on June 21, 2026.
How do I get to SoFi Stadium for World Cup 2026?
The most efficient method is LA Metro’s World Cup Match Day Direct Service, which runs from park-and-ride locations across Los Angeles. A dedicated shuttle from LAX is also available. SoFi Stadium is located in Inglewood, approximately 3 miles from Los Angeles International Airport.
Is SoFi Stadium hosting the 2028 Olympics?
Yes. SoFi Stadium is scheduled to host the Opening Ceremony of the 2028 Los Angeles Summer Olympics, making it the centrepiece of a consecutive run of global mega-events from the 2026 World Cup through the 2028 Games. It also hosts Super Bowl LXI in February 2027.
