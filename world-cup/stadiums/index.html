The most historically significant football stadium on earth. Home to Pelé’s 1970 World Cup masterclass, Maradona’s Hand of God and Goal of the Century in 1986, and now the opening match of 2026. The only stadium to host three World Cups. The altitude creates a unique playing environment — visiting teams struggle for the first 20 minutes as lungs adjust to the thinner air. Home nations benefit enormously from this advantage. Capacity reduced to 83,000 for 2026 after renovation works; also commercially known as Estadio Banorte.

FIFA Naming Policy: During the tournament, FIFA replaces all commercial stadium names with neutral city-based names (e.g. “AT&T Stadium” becomes “Dallas Stadium”). The Final — MetLife Stadium

MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey hosts the World Cup Final on July 19, 2026 — the centrepiece of the entire tournament. MetLife was chosen over Dallas and Los Angeles; Dallas was considered the frontrunner due to its 92,967 capacity and three-venue simulcast plans, while SoFi in Los Angeles was hampered by its narrow field dimensions and revenue-sharing disputes between owner Kroenke Sports & Entertainment and FIFA.

Halftime Show — A World Cup First

FIFA and Global Citizen co-produce the tournament’s first-ever Super Bowl-style halftime show at the Final. Headliners: Madonna, Shakira, and BTS. Duration: 11–15 minutes. Coldplay will assist with the performer list and organise a weekend concert in Times Square. The show aims to raise $100 million for the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund.

Infrastructure

Grass, Climate Control & Sustainability

Eight of the 16 venues normally use artificial turf and require a mandatory conversion to natural grass for the tournament. This is one of the largest logistical challenges of the entire event. The venues undergoing the conversion are: Atlanta, Boston, Dallas, Houston, Los Angeles, New Jersey, Seattle, and Vancouver. Temporary grass surfaces will be installed with built-in irrigation systems to avoid the performance and durability issues seen at the 2025 Club World Cup.

Climate-Controlled
Atlanta, Dallas, and Houston will operate with closed roofs and air conditioning during matches to combat extreme summer heat.
Heat Warning
Miami and Monterrey are expected to experience “sweltering, energy-sapping heat” — both open-air venues with no roof cover during peak summer.
Sustainability
Atlanta Stadium is recognised as the most sustainable stadium in the world. Estadio Monterrey was the first stadium in the Americas to earn LEED silver certification.
Regional Highlights

Moments That Make This Tournament Historic

🇲🇽
Estadio Azteca — The Only Three-Time World Cup Stadium
Mexico City Stadium becomes the first and only venue in history to host matches in three separate World Cups: 1970, 1986, and 2026. Sitting at 7,200 feet above sea level, altitude remains a decisive factor for all visiting teams.
🇺🇸
Philadelphia, July 4 — World Cup on the 250th Anniversary
Lincoln Financial Field hosts a Round of 16 match on July 4, 2026 — the 250th anniversary of American independence (Semiquincentennial). Projected to draw over 500,000 fans to the city. SEPTA will add 10 extra trains on the Broad Street Line and offer free post-match rides for up to two hours.
🇨🇦
Vancouver — World’s Largest Cable-Supported Retractable Roof
BC Place is the most northerly World Cup 2026 venue and the coolest in terms of climate. It features the world’s largest cable-supported retractable roof — a unique architectural feature that sets it apart from every other stadium in the tournament.
🇨🇦
Toronto — 17,000 Temporary Seats Added
BMO Field is being expanded with 17,000 temporary seats to meet FIFA’s tournament requirements, pushing capacity to 45,736. One of the most significant venue transformation projects in the entire 2026 hosting effort.
Getting There

Transit Highlights by Host City

Fan numbers in host cities are projected to exceed 500,000 for high-demand fixtures. Each city is deploying dedicated transit upgrades to manage the influx.

New York / New Jersey
NJ Transit shuttle train via the Meadowlands Rail Line. New $35M busway. Round-trip fare: $105 (reduced from $150 after sponsorships secured).
Philadelphia
SEPTA adding 10 extra trains on the Broad Street Line per match. Free rides home from NRG Station for up to 2 hours post-match.
Boston
Parking reduced from 20,000 to 5,000 spots to encourage transit. Options: $80 train service or $95 “Boston Stadium Express” bus from the city.
